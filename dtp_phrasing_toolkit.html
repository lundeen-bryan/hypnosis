Deep Trance Phenomena — Phrasing Toolkit

Light Theme

Use these phrasing clusters inside your visualizations (escalator, river, pond, sky, garden) to invite automatic, unconscious responses. Aim for slow pacing, soft tone, and frequent pauses.

What are Deep Trance Phenomena?

Deep Trance Phenomena (DTP) are experiences that arise when attention and imagination become so engaged that certain responses happen automatically — beyond deliberate effort. Examples include catalepsy (pleasant immobility), amnesia (temporary forgetting), time distortion, automatic movements, and perceptual changes (positive/negative hallucinations, analgesia). In practice, depth is less about how relaxed someone looks and more about their responsiveness to suggestion.

Quick How-To

  1. Pick a visualization (escalator → garden pond → river → sky).
  2. Sprinkle 1–2 phrasing lines from a relevant card every 20–30 seconds.
  3. Mark small changes (“that’s right… good…”) and add a soft test inside the story.
  4. When you get a result, praise it and continue the journey.
  5. End with integration and a clear emerge.

Tip: rehearse aloud until the cadence becomes automatic. Use Ctrl/Cmd + F in this page to jump to a DTP type on the fly.

Automatic Processes General

Invite experiences that happen by themselves (great with ideomotor drift or automatic swallowing/breathing changes).

Phrases
  • You don’t have to make it happen — it can begin to happen on its own.
  • Notice what your hand prefers to do all by itself.
  • Some changes start quietly, and then you only notice them after they’ve already begun.
  • It’s okay to be curious about which part moves first… or whether it just settles comfortably still.
  • As the scene continues, your responses can become pleasantly automatic.

Sensory Ambiguity Blur inner & outer

Soften distinctions to increase imaginative absorption (use with light cues, distant sounds, soft breezes).

Phrases
  • Perhaps that glow is outside you… or perhaps it’s forming within.
  • You might hear my voice as if from a little farther away… or as your own thoughts, repeating.
  • The more real it becomes, the less you need to decide how it’s real.
  • It can feel both familiar and new, as if remembered and imagined together.
  • You can notice… without needing to explain.

Dissociation & Amnesia Let go

Create gentle separation for forgetting, comfort, and selective attention.

Phrases
  • It’s as if you can watch yourself relaxing from a comfortable distance.
  • One part remembers everything perfectly, while another part can drift and rest.
  • Some thoughts float up and away before you even finish them.
  • Later, the useful parts can return in their own good time.
  • For now, you can set certain thoughts aside, like placing a book on a quiet shelf.

Time Distortion Elastic time

Invite expanded or compressed time — often appears naturally during immersive imagery.

Phrases
  • This moment can last as long as you’d like.
  • It might feel as if hours have passed… or only a few breaths.
  • As you look back later, you may wonder how long you were here.
  • Time outside can move normally while your inner time moves differently.
  • You can do a lot of inner work in just a little clock-time.

Catalepsy Stillness

Use gentle inevitability and comfort to invite immobility.

Phrases
  • As comfort settles in, some muscles can become so pleasantly at-ease that they don’t need to move yet.
  • You might notice a pause… as if the hand finds the perfect place to rest, too comfortable to move for now.
  • The more the mind drifts, the easier it is for the body to become quietly steady.
  • That easy stillness can simply remain, until it feels right to change.
  • You could try to move now… and discover you’d rather enjoy the stillness a little longer.

Ideomotor & Automatic Movement Micro-movements

Great for finger signals, arm levitation, or gentle sways.

Phrases
  • Sometimes a finger knows first — a tiny signal that appears before you decide.
  • You may be curious which hand grows lighter… or which prefers to rest heavier.
  • Imagine a soft thread lifting… and let your body discover what it wants to do with that image.
  • Notice how a breath can tip the balance… and something begins to move by itself.
  • Even not moving is a response — and that, too, can deepen your absorption.

Positive Hallucination Adding

Invite the addition of vivid sensory detail (best after strong imagery is established).

Phrases
  • As the scene becomes richer, notice what new detail appears first — a glint, a texture, a subtle sound.
  • If there were a pleasant fragrance here, what would it be like?
  • Let the picture sharpen a little, the way a camera finds focus.
  • Allow the experience to become just real enough that your body responds naturally.
  • It can be surprising how the mind supplies exactly what completes the scene.

Negative Hallucination Subtracting

Gently omit or fade an element — works well with “selective attention” frames.

Phrases
  • You can focus so well on this, that less important things fade to the background.
  • It’s easy to let certain details blur, as if they’re not needed for now.
  • Some sounds can become part of the background — like distant waves — while this stays clear.
  • If that thought were written in sand, watch the tide smooth it away.
  • For a while, it’s as if that thing just… isn’t there to consider.

Analgesia / Anesthesia Comfort

For ethical practice, always follow training and guidelines. Use comfort, numbness metaphors, temperature shifts.

Phrases
  • Imagine a cool, soothing breeze passing over that area… calming, comforting, easing.
  • Like dipping the hand into cool, clear water — sensations can soften and quiet.
  • Numbers on a dimmer can turn down — ten… to seven… to three… to whatever level is most comfortable now.
  • Comfort can spread outward, the way warmth from a mug moves through your fingers.
  • Any remaining sensation can be set aside, like turning the volume to a safe, quiet level.

Fractionation & Deepening Down & back

Cycle attention briefly up and back down to ratchet depth.

Phrases
  • In a moment, I’ll invite you to take a slightly deeper breath… and as you exhale, you can drift even further inward.
  • Eyes may want to flutter… and each time they settle, everything doubles in calm.
  • Coming up just enough to notice how good it feels to go back down again.
  • Each return carries you farther, like gentle waves traveling shoreward.
  • Good — and now, allow that comfort to fold over you once more.

Embedded Testing (Seed the DTP) Verify

Test softly inside the metaphor so the “test” feels like part of the story.

Phrases
  • As that hand rests perfectly still, you could try to lift it now… and notice it prefers to remain resting.
  • Search for the last number… and find that the mind skips lightly over it, like a stone over water.
  • Let a name drift behind the clouds for a while — there when needed, out of sight for now.
  • Count a few steps… and notice how easy it is to lose track in the comfort.
  • Good — the moment you notice the effect, it can deepen again.
