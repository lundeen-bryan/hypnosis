No More Advice Activity

(a.k.a. “If I Were You” Exercise)
From Cuddy Cudworth’s Udemy course: Become An Expert in Clean Language Techniques and Coaching
Back to Top
“It’s not the answer that enlightens, but the question.” — Eugène Ionesco

Purpose

This exercise helps participants experience the difference between giving advice and facilitating self‑generated insight. It demonstrates how Clean Language encourages others to find their own solutions and how advice can inadvertently impose the adviser’s perspective.

Setup

  • Pair up with a partner.
  • Each person chooses two small, real‑life problems suitable for exploration.
  • Alternate roles as speaker and listener.

Part 1 — The Advice Round

  1. Ask: “What’s a problem you’d like to solve?”
  2. Listen briefly.
  3. Respond with advice or solutions, for example:
    • “If I were you, I’d …”
    • “You should try …”
    • “Have you thought about … ?”
    • “What you need to do is …”
  4. Reflect afterward:
    • How did it feel to give advice?
    • Did your partner feel heard or interrupted?
    • Did your advice reflect your perspective rather than theirs?

Part 2 — The Clean Question Round

Ask your partner for a different small problem. This time: no advice, no suggestions. Stay with their words and experience. Use only the questions recommended by Cuddy for this exercise:

  • “What would you like to have happen?”
  • “What needs to happen for you to move forward?”
  • “Is there anything else that needs to happen for you to get a different result?”
Allow silence. Keep your tone gentle and curious. Let their answers emerge without steering.

Part 3 — Reflection (Partner)

  • Which approach helped you feel more understood?
  • Which led to deeper insight or motivation?
  • What did you notice in how you spoke or felt in each round?

Part 3 — Reflection (Self)

  • How difficult was it to resist giving advice?
  • Did you notice an urge to fix?
  • How did your partner’s thinking shift when you stayed with questions?

Why This Matters

  • Advice imposes your perspective and can limit growth.
  • Clean questions help others access their own inner resources.
  • Practicing this develops skill in listening, coaching, and facilitating change.

Optional Deepening Practice

  • Record both rounds and notice differences in tone and pacing.
  • Observe moments when you were tempted to add advice.
  • Practice asking only these three questions and allowing silence for reflection.
