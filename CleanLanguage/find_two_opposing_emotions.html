Clean Language — Find Two Opposing Emotions

A pocket process to surface the problem state and the resource state, ready for Mirroring Hands or integration.

1

Start with Outcome

“What would you like to have happen?”
2

Explore the Problem Feeling

“What kind of feeling is that feeling?”
“And where is that feeling?”
“And what happens next?”
3

Name It Gently

“And if that feeling had a name, what might it be called?” → problem state
4

Invite the Contrast

“And when that [problem feeling] isn’t there, what happens instead?”
“What would you like to feel instead?” → resource state
5

Deepen the Resource

“And what kind of [resource feeling] is that [resource feeling]?”
“And where is that [resource feeling]?”
“And how will you know it’s the right [resource feeling] for you?”
6

Confirm Both States

“So there’s [problem state]… and there’s [resource state]. Is that right?”
Next: move to your preferred integration (e.g., Mirroring Hands / ideomotor).

Rapid Integration (Mirroring Hands cue)

“Perhaps one hand can represent [problem state]… and the other [resource state]… and you can just notice how they begin to respond… and when they know to come together, your unconscious can discover a new balance that’s right for you.”

Tip: Keep tone slow and permissive. Stay inside their exact words. When imagery or body-sense becomes vivid, you’re already at the threshold of trance.
