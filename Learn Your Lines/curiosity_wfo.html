Curiosity Frame – Hypnosis Practice

Well-Formed Outcome for Learning Through Curiosity

A simple inner framework to help you trust your unconscious mind, return to curiosity, and treat every hypnosis session as a chance to learn.

◎Pre-Frame: Why I’m Here

Chosen before the session begins.

Hypnosis is easy and intriguing because it lets me discover what I’m capable of as a hypnotist. I’m not here to perform or impress anyone. I’m here to see what I can learn and what my unconscious mind will show me.

This is the mental doorway I step through each time, shifting from performance to exploration.

★Core Well-Formed Outcome

My outcome is to let my words flow naturally and trust my unconscious mind to teach me, using each session as a chance to follow my curiosity and discover something new.

This outcome is fully under my control. I measure it internally by whether I stay in, or return to, a state of curiosity about my own inner experience, regardless of how the subject responds.

Success is about my state and learning, not anyone else’s reaction.

Inner Attitude

✶Supporting Affirmations

Soft reminders I can read or recall before a session.

  • I’m always curious about what I can learn.
  • I’m intrigued by the words that naturally flow out of my mouth.
  • My inner experience is more important than what happens outside.
  • I treat every session as a chance to learn something new.
  • I trust my unconscious mind to teach me in its own way.

These affirmations support the outcome by keeping my focus on learning, not performance.

☼Belief Set for Curiosity & Stability

Guiding frames I hold during sessions.

  • First responses are not final: I seldom take a person’s first response to hypnosis as written in stone. It reflects their momentary thoughts, feelings, or beliefs — and it’s almost always subject to change.
  • Sovereignty of attention: People can respond however they respond; that’s their freedom. I choose where my energy goes, and I choose what I learn from every situation, event, circumstance, or action.
  • Permission & uncertainty: If I can walk with uncertainty without needing a guarantee, and give the other person radical permission to have their own first response, something wonderful and transformational can happen.
  • Not-knowing invites learning: Sometimes it is easier and more rewarding to not know and to embrace uncertainty. It is only in the state of not knowing that we are open to learning.
  • Act to find out: Not knowing what will happen no longer means I must stop or wait. It means I choose to act, to find out — changing the meaning of not-knowing into movement and discovery.
Activation & State Shifts

◆Activation Ritual with the Ring

Before a session, I put on my ring. As I make a gentle fist, I imagine the ring catching the light and shining. In my mind, I hear the clear sound of swords clashing: “shwing!” That sound activates the ring.

In that moment, I imagine a blue energy forming in my solar plexus, where fear used to sit. The blue feeling grows there and begins to rise upward. As it rises, it softens any tension and turns into a small spark of curiosity.

I think to myself: “What can I learn today? I’m really curious what my unconscious mind will show me.”

This ritual marks the shift into my learning-through-curiosity state.

ΨHandling Fear During a Session

If fear appears during the session and I feel that sharp sensation in my solar plexus, I notice it and remember that I have options. Instead of seeing it as a mistake, I think: “Oh, interesting… here is an opportunity to redirect the subject and to see what my unconscious mind will do.”

I thank my unconscious mind for noticing the fear and being willing to redirect me. I imagine the blue energy forming again in my solar plexus, rising up and out as new words. As it rises, I feel a spark of curiosity and hear myself think: “Oh, that’s interesting…”

I simply trust my unconscious and say the words that come.

Returning to curiosity is the correction, not saying the “perfect” line.

Centering in Facts & Choice

Fact: I want to hypnotize someone.

Fact: I don’t know what will happen.

Fact: I don’t like what I don’t know.

Choice: I choose to find out anyway.

Success & Integration

✓Definition of Success

Success is not about putting the subject into a deep trance or getting good feedback. Those things are outside my control.

Success is defined very simply: if I notice fear or pressure and I am able to return to curiosity about my own mind and my own learning, the session is a success.

At the end of each session, I reinforce this by asking myself: “What did my unconscious show me today?” Whatever comes to mind, no matter how small, is accepted as evidence of learning.

Over time, this builds a strong, unconscious belief: every experience teaches me something useful.
