Demystifying Hypnosis — Pre-talk Handout

▶I. Prior Experience

“Have you ever been hypnotized before?”

If yes: “How was that experience for you? Did you enjoy it? ” (Listen. Note preconceptions, positives, concerns.)

If no: “Great — we’ll keep it simple and natural. I’ll guide you step‑by‑step.”

▶II. Introduction

“Hypnosis for most people is what they see on a stage show, where they see someone shout ‘SLEEP!’ and the person suddenly goes limp. But hypnosis isn’t actually sleep — it’s a heightened state of awareness and focus called trance.”

“So when I say the word ‘sleep’, I won’t be telling you to go to sleep. I’ll simply be guiding you into that comfortable, relaxed, but focused state of awareness that that you previously experienced.”

“Would you believe me if I said that we’re experiencing trance most of the time?”

▶III. Metaphors

A. Raise your hand if you've ever been driving and realized you missed your exit because your mind drifted? — “Maybe you were listening to the radio or daydreaming a little. You’re still aware of what’s going on on the road, but just enough of your attention is on something else that you miss your exit.”

You know who Milton Erickson is, right?
(nods softly)
He once said, “Trance is a natural everyday experience.”
(soft nod continues)
That’s right… let me give you another example.

B. What happens when I ask you, “How many windows are in your house?” — “Notice how you have to go inside your imagination to count each window in the house while another part stays here listening. That natural shift of focus is the same kind of experience we use in hypnosis.”

C. Raise your hand if you’ve ever gone to the movies in the afternoon—bright daylight when you walk in—and when you come out it’s pitch-black night. — “It’s funny, isn’t it? For a moment you almost forget how much time has gone by. That’s hypnosis, because you were so engaged in that story—so focused on what was happening on the screen—that the rest of the world just faded out for a while.”

D. Have you ever been so absorbed in a book or movie that you lost track of time? — “That absorption is similar to how trance feels.”

E. Have you ever caught yourself daydreaming and noticed the world fading into the background? — “That gentle drifting is the same kind of experience we use in hypnosis.”

F. Have you ever listened to music so deeply that everything else seemed to disappear? — “That focused absorption is perfect for this process.”

G. Have you ever found yourself in the zone while walking or exercising, where your body moves almost automatically? — “That comfortable rhythm is the same kind of focus we’ll use now.”

H. Have you noticed those moments just before sleep when thoughts wander and images appear on their own? — “Hypnosis is like that in-between state — aware, yet pleasantly absorbed.”

I. Have you ever been so engaged in a conversation or hobby that time seemed to fly by? — “That natural time-distortion is common in trance as well.”

▶IV. Bridge into Induction Example:

I have one more very interesting example that you should find fun to imagine, but for this I'll ask you to close your eyes for just a moment...

Imagine that a good friend has given you a fresh lemon from their tree... in fact! it still has the stem on it! It's a beautiful, bright yellow lemon with a nice, shiny skin, and it's actually such a big lemon that it takes two hands to hold it. You can feel the skin on the lemon between your hands and just to find out what happens imagine holding it up to your nose and smelling it? What happens?

(wait for response — this gauges how they respond to sensory suggestions)

Now let's take this lemon to your kitchen and get out a cutting board. Put it on the cutting board and imagine cutting it in half. Notice all the juices coming out of this lemon! Now... hold one half of the lemon up to your mouth and take a big bite out of it and tell me what you notice... that's right.

Just take a moment to appreciate that experience and when you're ready just bring yourself back and we'll have a little chat about it.

▶V. Common Concerns about Hypnosis

“There are usually three—no, actually four—common concerns that people have about hypnosis.”

“The first is about sleep — and we already talked about that. Hypnosis isn’t sleep, it’s that focused, aware state we practiced earlier.”

“The second is, ‘What if I get stuck inside and can’t get out?’ — but you already proved to yourself with the lemon experience that you can come back easily and naturally anytime you choose.”

“And sometimes people ask, ‘What if I reveal something I don’t mean to say or do something I don’t want to do?’ The mind has a built-in filter called the critical faculty. It’s like a gate that keeps anything from going in or out that you don’t want. You hold the key, so you’ll never say or do anything that goes against your values or intentions. Does that make sense? ” (nod head)

▶VI. Invite Questions

“Do you have any questions about hypnosis?”

▶VII. Seating Properly

Sit back from the camera so I can see more of you all the way back, so I can see your hands and where they are resting comfortably.

We're going to continue these little exercises to help us get warmed up to hypnosis, so I'll ask you a few more questions. Is that okay?
