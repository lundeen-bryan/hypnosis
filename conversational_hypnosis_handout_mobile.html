Framing + Magic Word + SPE/Suggestion

Formula

Framing Statement + Magic Word + SPE (State/Process/Experience) OR Suggestion
Use framing to open the door, a magic word to soften/prime, then land a state or suggestion.

Page 1 · Framing Statements

A framing statement sets the stage for an indirect suggestion by orienting attention and reducing resistance.

When you…
“When you sit comfortably, you may notice your body beginning to relax.”
What would it be like if…
“What would it be like if you felt a wave of calm spreading through you?”
A person can…
“A person can easily drift into relaxation when they hear a soothing voice.”
If you were to…
“If you were to imagine floating, how would that feel in your body?”
As you…
“As you listen, your mind may wander to pleasant places.”
It’s not necessary to…
“It’s not necessary to go deeply into trance right away… you can take your time.”
You really shouldn’t…
“You really shouldn’t relax too quickly… and yet people often do.”
You might find…
“You might find that your eyes feel heavier with every blink.”
To the point where…
“Your comfort can deepen to the point where relaxation becomes automatic.”
Invite you to notice…
“I invite you to notice the sense of calm in your breathing.”
How surprised would you be to…
“How surprised would you be to notice your hand beginning to lift by itself?”

Page 3 · Full Examples (Formula in Action)

“When you naturally become aware of your breathing slowing, you can relax more deeply now.”
“What would it be like if you easily allowed your mind to drift beyond ordinary awareness and discovered new calm inside?”
“As you automatically notice your eyes blinking slower, you might find them closing all by themselves.”
“It’s not necessary to consciously try, because your unconscious naturally creates the right level of relaxation.”
“You might notice that as you experience comfort spreading through your body, change becomes unlimited in its possibilities.”
“I invite you to notice how easily your thoughts expand beyond effort, and you begin to feel lighter.”
“How surprised would you be to realize that your hand automatically starts to lift, as if by itself?”
