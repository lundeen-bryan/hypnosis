Pendulum‑Guided Self‑Hypnosis — First‑Person Procedure

This version uses a first‑person Ericksonian induction with a guiding metaphor to align my mind for pendulum work.

Five Steps

  1. Before trance: I read my printed suggestion checklist once from top to bottom. No evaluation, no picking winners—just exposure so my conscious mind relaxes.
  2. Induction: I sit comfortably, feet on the floor, hands resting. I breathe evenly and use any familiar method (eye fixation, a slow 10→1 count‑down, or progressive relaxation) to enter a calm, focused state.
  3. Pendulum setup: I hold the pendulum lightly between thumb and forefinger. I ask my unconscious to show a clear yes movement and then a clear no movement. I thank my unconscious when the signals are distinct.
  4. Test each statement: I read one suggestion slowly. I pause and observe the pendulum. When it indicates yes, I mark that item on my checklist; if no or neutral, I move on without forcing meaning.
  5. After the session: I review the marked items and notice the theme. I use those as my focal suggestions for rehearsal, recording, or gentle repetition.

I usually select two to five items. If more are marked, I circle the few that feel most relevant right now.

How I Use the Pendulum

I rest my elbow so the hand stays relaxed and let the pendulum hang freely. I say, silently or aloud: “Unconscious mind, show me a clear signal for ‘yes’.” I observe the movement (for example, front‑to‑back). I let it settle. I ask for “no” and notice the different movement (for example, side‑to‑side). If signals are unclear, I invite: “Make ‘yes’ stronger… thank you. Make ‘no’ clearer… thank you.” When they are distinct, I say: “When I read a suggestion that would best strengthen my self‑esteem today, move in the ‘yes’ direction. If not ideal, move ‘no’ or remain still.”

First‑Person Ericksonian Induction (with Metaphor)

I take an easy breath in… and let the exhale be just a little longer. With each out‑breath, I allow unnecessary effort to drain from my face, my jaw, my shoulders, my hands. I don’t have to make anything happen; I can simply let what wants to settle… settle.

As I breathe, I imagine a quiet lake at dawn. The surface is still, and a single leaf floats there, steady and at ease. Thoughts can ripple and pass, and the water becomes calm again—because calm is its natural state. My attention can be like that water: clear, reflective, honest.

Whether I notice it now or in a few breaths, I can allow a comfortable focus to gather in the center of my chest. My body knows how to relax; my mind knows how to pay attention. I’m becoming aware of a simple truth: when I am honest with myself, I already move in the right direction.

Now I picture a compass settling—first it swings, then it steadies, pointing where it needs to point. My pendulum will mirror that wisdom in my hands. I don’t need to push the needle; I allow alignment. I allow clarity. I allow the signal to become obvious when it matters.

I say inwardly: “I’m entering a receptive, truthful state. I invite my unconscious to guide my choices for strengthening authentic self‑esteem today. Let ‘yes’ be clear, let ‘no’ be clear, and let neutrality be simple.”

Now I begin a slow count from 10 down to 1. With each number, I settle a little deeper into honest ease. 10… 9… 8… each breath softens the body; 7… 6… 5… my attention becomes steady and open; 4… 3… 2… and at 1, I am ready to cooperate with myself fully.

I hold the pendulum lightly. I confirm my signals: yes… no… and stillness. I test the first suggestion, allowing the movement to appear in its own time. I let the answers be simple. I let the process be clean. I accept what aligns, and I release what does not—today.

When selection feels complete, I breathe in gently and say: “These choices are accepted.” I count up 1… 2… 3… 4… 5, returning clear and refreshed, bringing with me a calm certainty about what to practice next.

After I emerge, I rehearse each chosen suggestion three times aloud, unhurried, imprinting tone and meaning.
