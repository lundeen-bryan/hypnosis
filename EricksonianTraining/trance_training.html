Trance Training — Definition and Process

Definition

Trance Training is a structured process in Ericksonian hypnosis where the subject is guided to directly experience and explore trance states—learning how attention, perception, and responsiveness shift during hypnosis. Its purpose is to demystify trance, increase comfort and confidence, and develop flexible awareness that supports deeper, more effective hypnotic work.

I. Framing and Orientation

Purpose: Explain trance in everyday terms and remove mystery or anxiety.
Example statements
  • “Trance isn’t something strange or mystical—it’s a natural state you already enter when you daydream or get lost in a movie.”
  • “Today we’ll explore what that state feels like for you, so you can recognize it and use it intentionally.”
  • “You’ll stay aware and in control the whole time. The goal is simply to learn what trance is, from your own experience.”

II. The Unconscious Protector Set

Purpose: Reassure the conscious mind that the unconscious will protect safety and well-being throughout the process, allowing the conscious mind to relax and cooperate.
Function
  • Establishes psychological safety by reducing fear of losing control.
  • Encourages cooperation between conscious and unconscious processes.
  • Prevents overwhelm by affirming that only safe and useful experiences will occur.
  • Reinforces that hypnosis is a natural collaboration, not a surrender of will.
Example statements
  • “As we begin, it’s important to know that your unconscious mind will automatically protect you throughout this experience. It always has your safety and well-being in mind.”
  • “While you allow yourself to relax and follow along, your deeper mind will take care of you—so you can explore freely, knowing you’ll only experience what’s right and useful for you.”
  • “Even as you listen and allow your focus to drift inward, your unconscious mind keeps you safe, just as it keeps your heart beating and your lungs breathing.”
  • “You don’t need to make anything happen consciously; your unconscious mind will guide and protect you in the way that’s best for you right now.”

III. Guided Experience of Trance

Purpose: Allow the subject to directly experience shifts in attention and awareness.
Process
  1. Invite them to focus on something neutral—perhaps the sound of your voice or the rhythm of their breathing.
  2. Gradually narrow attention:
    • “As you listen to my voice, you might begin to notice the subtle rhythm of your breathing.”
    • “And as you notice that, part of your mind can simply drift inward.”
  3. Use sensory and spatial language to evoke inner focus:
    • “You might begin to sense a change in the quality of your thoughts, as if they slow down or spread out.”
    • “And some people find that their body feels lighter, or heavier, or just comfortably still.”

IV. Reflection and Integration

Purpose: Help the subject recognize and name the experience of trance.
Example questions
  • “What did you notice happening inside as you focused inward?”
  • “How did your awareness of the room change?”
  • “Was there a moment you realized you were more absorbed in your inner world than before?”
Follow-up statements
  • “That shift you just described—that’s what we call trance.”
  • “The more familiar you become with it, the easier it becomes to enter that state when you choose.”

V. Self-Induction and Skill Building

Purpose: Encourage active participation and ownership of trance.
Steps
  1. Ask the subject to recreate the state intentionally:
    • “If you were to go back into that same relaxed inward focus, how would you begin?”
  2. Reinforce their success:
    • “Notice how easily you can return to that state now that you know what it feels like.”
  3. Normalize variability:
    • “Each trance can feel a little different. That’s perfectly natural—it shows you’re learning to use your mind in flexible ways.”

VI. Linking to Future Hypnotic Work

Purpose: Connect this learning to future sessions and build trust.
Statements
  • “This ability to shift attention inward is the same skill we’ll use in later sessions to create change.”
  • “Now that you’ve experienced trance for yourself, you know it’s simply a natural, safe process.”
  • “Whenever we do hypnosis together, you’ll recognize that familiar feeling of inward focus.”
