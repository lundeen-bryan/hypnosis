Quotes Often Mistaken for “Everything is Trance”

These are verified or closely paraphrased statements by Milton H. Erickson and his co-authors that express the same idea: trance as a natural, everyday, pervasive experience.

“Trance, in fact, is a natural state experienced by everyone.”
Attributed to Milton H. Erickson (commonly cited in training materials and quote collections; derived from Hypnotic Realities, 1976, and Experiencing Hypnosis, 1981).
“Without quite realizing it, we all experience the ‘common everyday trance’ wherein we are absorbed in a moment of inner reverie or preoccupation.”
Milton H. Erickson & Ernest L. Rossi, Hypnotic Realities (Irvington, 1976), p. 17.
“Erickson has described the state of response attentiveness as the common ‘everyday trance,’ and he frequently utilizes this natural form of absorption just as he would trance.”
Milton H. Erickson & Ernest L. Rossi, Experiencing Hypnosis (Irvington, 1981), pp. 222–223.
“Even without any form of formal trance induction… ‘Suggestions’ made while the patient is in this state of absorption can be just as effective as when in formal trance. Trance thus need not be formally induced for effective clinical work.”
Milton H. Erickson & Ernest L. Rossi, Experiencing Hypnosis (Irvington, 1981), p. 223.
“[In everyday life] consciousness is in a continual state of flux between the general reality orientation and the momentary microdynamics of trance.”
Milton H. Erickson & Ernest L. Rossi, Hypnotic Realities (Irvington, 1976), p. 220–221.
“Erickson understands trance as a normal experience of everyday life that occurs naturally whenever a person becomes deeply absorbed in some inner or outer reality (e.g., daydreaming or listening to music).”
Milton H. Erickson & Ernest L. Rossi, Experiencing Hypnosis (Irvington, 1981), p. 222.

Note: None of these lines literally state “everything is trance.” That modern paraphrase summarizes Erickson’s principle that trance is a normal, naturally occurring aspect of everyday human experience.
