The Success Set in Ericksonian Hypnosis

What it is

The Success Set is a structured pre-hypnosis conversation that aligns a client’s conscious goals with their unconscious resources. It creates a mental and emotional “set” that primes the mind for success before formal trance work begins.

Purpose

Establish a positive expectancy frame by guiding the client to recall, embody, and symbolize qualities of success they already possess — bridging conscious intention with unconscious competence.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

1) Identify the Goal

Question: “What is an important goal for you right now?”

Clarifies direction and gives the unconscious mind a target.

2) Elicit the Motivation

Question: “Why is that important for you?”

Uncovers the deeper value that fuels the goal.

3) Presuppose Past Success

Statement: “My intuition says that you’ve experienced this before.”

Assumes capability and activates memories of competence.

4) Explore Identity

Question: “When you experienced this, what kind of person were you?”

Accesses who they were being when success occurred.

5) Create a Symbol (Animal Metaphor)

Question: “When you’re like that, which animal comes to mind?”

Builds a vivid shorthand for the successful mindset.

6) Summarize the State

Summary: “You want to be [identity/quality] and really feel [emotion/state].”

Integrates everything into a concise verbal anchor.

Conceptual View

  • Cognitive: Clarifies goals and motivations.
  • Emotional: Connects to feelings of achievement and fulfillment.
  • Symbolic: Encodes success through metaphor and imagery.
  • Hypnotic: Creates expectancy and readiness for deeper trance work.

Relation to Ericksonian Principles

  • Utilization: Use the client’s own words and experiences.
  • Presupposition: Assume capability and prior success.
  • Metaphor: Translate experiences into symbols for unconscious communication.
  • Pacing and Leading: Move from current experience to desired outcome.

In Practice

Treat the Success Set as a mini‑induction that naturally leads into trance. By the time you offer the summary, the client is often already in light trance, vividly connected to their success identity.

“You want to be calm, focused, and really feel that centered confidence — just like that tiger you mentioned — strong, relaxed, and ready to act.”
