Creating Interesting Pacing Statements

From the practice exercises in Mike Mandel's Hypnosis Academy (MMHA), Lesson 3.

Goal: Turn ordinary truisms into engaging, sensory, and permissive pacing statements.

How to Begin

  1. Pair up with a partner.
  2. Choose a simple fact about your partner or the environment. Examples: “You’re sitting in a chair.” “You’re breathing.” “You can hear my voice.”
  3. Say it three different ways—each time softer and more hypnotic:
    • Add sensory words: notice, feel, sense, be aware of.
    • Add permissive phrases: you might, it’s possible to, you can begin to.
    • Add pacing cues: as you sit there, while you’re breathing, right now.
  4. Ask for feedback on which version felt smoothest or most engaging.
  5. Switch roles and repeat.

Example Dialogue

Student A: “Okay, I’ll start simple. Your feet are on the floor.”

Student B: “Okay.”

Student A: “Let me make that more hypnotic… You can notice the feeling of the floor supporting your feet.”

Student B: “That one feels softer.”

Student A: “Good. How about this… As you’re sitting there, it’s possible to become aware of how your feet rest exactly where they are.”

Student B: “Yeah, that one helps me feel it.”

Student A: “Nice. Your turn.”

Practice Tips

  • Speak slowly and gently—pacing is about inviting awareness.
  • Keep attention on something real and present.
  • Gradually make statements more sensory or experiential.
  • Debrief: What phrasing felt best? What did you notice?

Optional Challenge

“As you sit there… noticing your breathing… and the way your feet rest on the floor…”

Then add a gentle lead:

“…you can begin to feel even more comfortable now.”
