Weekend Hypnosis Practice Plan

🧭 Pre-Session Plan and Announcements

  • While waiting for everyone to arrive, keep the energy friendly and conversational — welcome people, ask how they’re doing, and invite them to get comfortable.
  • If one of the regular facilitators joins, politely say:
    “Hey, would you mind if I take the driver’s seat for just a little while? Please feel free to step in if you feel I’ve crossed any line.”
  • Ask the group:
    “Would everyone be okay if we record the session for later review and feedback?”
    (If anyone objects, pause recording — always keep consent explicit.)
  • Once everyone’s ready, begin by saying:
    “I’m about to do an icebreaker. Does anyone know what an icebreaker is?”
    (Pause for responses — smile and nod.)
    Then: “Exactly — it’s just something fun to help everyone relax, connect, and get in the right state of mind.”
  • Then continue into the Richard Bandler Ice Breaker below.
  • After the icebreaker, transition naturally into the dolphin story and explain the positive-feedback rule.
  • Finally, announce that you’ll start off hypnotizing someone for today’s practice.

🌀 Richard Bandler Ice Breaker

Script

Alright, raise your hand if you know who Richard Bandler is.

(pause for hands — smile, nod)

Perfect. So this is a quick little exercise that comes straight from him. It’s fun, simple, and it’ll show you something cool about how your mind works.

Everyone, take a really quick deep breath through your mouth — like you’re sipping air through a straw.

(Demonstrate the “swwwwwithp” sound.)

Good.

Now we’ll do it one more time. But this time, as you take that breath, imagine a smile starting down at your feet, moving up through your legs, through your body, all the way up to your face.

And as it reaches your mouth, let it reach your teeth too — because as Bandler’s wife told him, it’s not really a smile without your teeth.

Ready? On three — one, two, three — swwwwwithp!

Nice! You can feel that, right?

So if you just made yourself feel happy, even a little, you just learned something: you can make yourself happy for no reason at all.

Isn’t that a cool thing?

(nod, smile)

🐬 The Dolphin Training Metaphor for Positive Feedback

Story + Rule

Did you know that dolphin trainers never punish mistakes?

When they’re teaching dolphins to do those amazing jumps and spins, they don’t scold them or blow a whistle when they get it wrong. They simply stay neutral.

But the moment the dolphin does something close to what they want, it gets a fish, a cheer, and a lot of attention.

Over time, the dolphin learns faster, because it feels safe to experiment. If the trainers ever punished mistakes, the dolphin would stop trying altogether.

So today, we’re taking that same approach.

Feedback will always sound like, “I liked how you…” That way, we’re giving each other positive reinforcement for the efforts we make.

If you want some critical feedback, just say so — we’ll be happy to give it when you ask.

But otherwise, today’s goal is to flood each other with praise and attention for everything that went right.

Because that’s how we all learn faster — and enjoy the process even more.
