Trance Depth & Communication Guide

Observable signs, best language choices, and when metaphors & stories work
Trance Level Observable Indicators Conscious Awareness Metaphor (Y/N) Story (Y/N) Best Communication Modes Purpose / Effect
1. Waking / Conversational Absorption Eye contact steady; nods or verbal replies; even breathing. Fully alert; analytical; inner dialogue active. Yes Yes Conversational metaphor; pacing & leading; light story fragments; humor. Build rapport; seed ideas; bypass light resistance.
2. Light Trance Slower blinks; softened facial tone; rhythmic breathing; still hands. Aware of environment (“I hear the dog”) yet inwardly focused. Yes Yes Guided imagery; symbolic stories; permissive phrasing (“You might find…”). Deepen trance; evoke curiosity; initiate re-association.
3. Medium Trance Eyes closed; delayed responses; slower respiration; minimal movement; time distortion. Diminished external awareness; vivid inner imagery; mild amnesia possible. Yes Yes Emotional metaphor; full narrative story; indirect suggestions; reframing. Deliver unconscious learning; evoke emotional re-patterning.
4. Deep Trance / Somnambulism Total stillness; shallow breathing; ideomotor signals (finger twitch, nod); fixed expression. Conscious mind largely dissociated; minimal inner dialogue. Limited No Direct suggestion; ideomotor signaling; symbolic/sensory anchors. Install post-hypnotic cues; test unconscious acceptance.
5. Profound / Esdaile (Coma) State Nearly catatonic stillness; very slow pulse & breath; unresponsive to voice. Conscious mind absent; unconscious fully dominant. No No Silence; touch anchors; tone/prosody; nonverbal cues. Analgesia; profound rest; nonverbal healing.

Notes: Metaphors and stories are most effective in light to medium trance, where awareness flickers between outer and inner focus. In deeper levels, favor direct suggestion and ideomotor work.
