Trance Training in Ericksonian Hypnosis

Definition

Trance Training refers to the progressive conditioning of the conscious and unconscious mind to recognize, enter, and use hypnotic states with increasing ease and flexibility. In the Ericksonian model, this process cultivates cooperation between conscious and unconscious processes, making trance a natural, everyday skill for learning, healing, and creativity.

Examples of Trance Training Exercises

# Exercise Description Example Phrase Training Goal
1 Fractionation Practice Moving in and out of trance repeatedly to strengthen depth and responsiveness. Now, open your eyes and stretch… and as you let them close again, notice how much easier it is to drift inside. Build trance depth and unconscious familiarity.
2 Sensory Awareness Exercises Developing sensitivity to inner experience and physical sensations. Notice how the air feels on your skin… or how your breathing naturally adjusts as you focus inward. Enhance sensory focus and self‑awareness.
3 Utilization of Everyday Trance Recognizing and amplifying naturally occurring trance states. When you’re absorbed in a good book—that’s already trance. Notice that feeling and deepen it. Normalize trance and make it accessible anytime.
4 Hypnotic Phenomena Practice Experimenting with responses like hand levitation, time distortion, or amnesia. As your hand begins to lift on its own, you can let that surprise deepen your trance. Build unconscious cooperation and responsiveness.
5 Dual Awareness (Dissociation) Drills Dividing attention between inner and outer awareness to train flexibility of focus. As you listen to my voice, part of you can notice the words… and another part can wander somewhere pleasant inside. Develop split‑attention control for conversational work.
6 Self‑Hypnosis Conditioning Creating personal cues or anchors to enter trance independently. Each time you take a slow breath and close your eyes, you can return to that same calm, resourceful place. Encourage self‑directed trance for daily use.
7 Pre‑Closure Phenomena Seeding Describing deep‑trance phenomena before eye closure so the description itself begins to create the experience. Before you even close your eyes, you can notice how time can feel pleasantly stretchy… how sounds fade to the edges while your attention gathers inside… and as your body remembers that heavy‑light, floating‑settling sensation, you might realize it’s already beginning. Evokes phenomena through expectancy and guided imagination.
