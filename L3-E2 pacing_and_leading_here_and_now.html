Pacing and Leading the Here and Now

From Mike Mandel's Hypnosis Academy (MMHA), Lesson 3, Exercise 2.

Goal: Guide present-moment awareness using pacing (undeniable observations) and gentle leading. This is not an invitation into trance—just real-time attention shaping.

How to Begin

  1. Partner up. One is the hypnotist; the other is the subject.
  2. Start with three pacing statements that are obviously true right now. Examples: “You’re sitting in a chair.” “You can hear my voice.” “You’re breathing in and out.”
  3. Add one or two leads that direct awareness without aiming for trance. Examples: “You might notice how steady your breathing is.” “You may feel a little more curious about what’s next.”
  4. Watch for subtle responses: a small breath change, posture shift, or visual focus.
  5. Switch roles and repeat.

Example Conversation

Student A (Hypnotist): “We’ll begin with pacing. You’re seated in your chair. Your feet are resting on the floor. You can hear my voice right now.”

Student B (Subject): “Yes.”

Student A: “Now a gentle lead: You might notice how steady your breathing feels in this moment.”

Student B: “I do notice that.”

Student A: “And as you notice that steadiness, you might find yourself a bit more focused on what’s happening here and now.”

Student B: “Yes, I feel that.”

Student A: “Great—that’s the aim: guiding attention, not inducing trance.”

What to Observe

  • Small shifts in breathing, facial muscles, or posture.
  • Eyes becoming more still or more engaged with a point of focus.
  • Faster, clearer responses to leads (“Yeah,” “I notice that”).

Debrief

  • Which pacing statements felt most natural to say and hear?
  • Which leads smoothly directed attention without implying trance?
  • What subtle changes signaled that the lead was followed?

Compare with “Invitation into Trance”

Both use pacing → leading. The difference is the target: here, the lead aims at present-moment awareness (not relaxation or deepening). In Invitation into Trance, leads intentionally nudge toward trance states.
