Pre‑Talk Calibration Toolkit

Pick 2–3 quick checks during your pre‑talk to both teach and test trance familiarity. No scripts — just natural conversation.

How to use: Ask one question, watch and listen. If you get a clear “yes” — verbal or non‑verbal — make a brief utilization link (e.g., “Just like that, you can imagine…”) and move forward.
Visual • Imagery

How many windows are in your house?

Calibrates
Visualization, eye movement/closure, internal mapping.
Positive response
Eyes shift/close, counting aloud, “walking through” rooms.
Use later: “Just like you pictured each room, picture a calm place now…”
Visual • Memory

Can you picture your childhood bedroom?

Calibrates
Recall + imagery of environments; detail tolerance.
Positive response
Nostalgic smile, far‑away gaze, descriptive specifics.
Auditory • Selective Attention

In a noisy room, have you noticed your name pops out?

Calibrates
Unconscious filtering; automatic attention shifts.
Positive response
Quick nod, “Yeah, all the time,” widened eyes.
Auditory • Replay

Ever had a song stuck in your head?

Calibrates
Automatic auditory loops; ease of internal sound.
Positive response
Laugh, head shake, humming or quoting lyrics.
Kinesthetic • Mind–Body

Before a test or talk, did your stomach feel it?

Calibrates
Awareness that thoughts affect sensations.
Positive response
Hand to stomach, nod, “Absolutely.”
Kinesthetic • Sensory Recall

Can you feel warm shower water just by remembering it?

Calibrates
Sensory imagination; comfort access.
Positive response
Relaxed breath, softening shoulders, “Yes.”
Trance Phenomena • Time

Ever get so absorbed that hours flew by?

Calibrates
Familiarity with time distortion; focused absorption.
Positive response
Personal example, recognition smile.
Trance Phenomena • Automaticity

On a long drive, ever “miss a bit” of the trip?

Calibrates
Everyday trance; automatic behaviors.
Positive response
Laughter, “Oh yes,” relaxed posture.
Trance Phenomena • Absorption

Have you ever been driving and missed your exit because your mind drifted?

Calibrates
Everyday trance; focused absorption with automatic behavior.
Positive response
Laugh, “Yep—more than once,” brief story, far‑away gaze.
Utilize: “That same kind of natural focus lets you drift comfortably while still safe and aware here.”
Paradox • Utilization

The harder you try not to laugh…

Calibrates
Acceptance of paradox; how resistance can assist trance.
Positive response
Grin, chuckle, immediate agreement.
Utilize: “That same paradox helps some people drift deeper when they try to stay ‘too’ focused.”
Paradox • Negation Effect

Don’t think of a pink elephant…

Calibrates
Susceptibility to suggestion via negation.
Positive response
Laugh, “I pictured it instantly.”
Quick Utilization Prompts
Link the experience
“Just like you did a moment ago…” • “In the same way you pictured those windows…”
Invite the shift
“As you remember that feeling, let the body settle 5% more now.”
Mark compliance
“That’s it.” • “Good.” • “Exactly.”
Myth-Busting Story • 2‑minute version

The Curious Cat and the Box (Refined)

I have a story for you. Once upon a time, there was a little cat who was curious about boxes but also a little afraid of them.

The little cat had three fears about going inside the box:

  1. “What if I get stuck inside and can’t get out?”
  2. “What if the box makes me spill my secrets?”
  3. “What if the box makes me do something silly or weird?”

One day, an older cat smiled and said, “Boxes don’t do any of that. You’re the one who chooses when to go in and when to come out. Inside, it’s just a space to imagine and explore. You never lose control, your secrets remain yours, and you’ll never do anything that doesn’t fit who you are.”

The little cat thought about it, then bravely jumped into the box. After a few moments, she hopped right back out again — safe, smiling, and more confident than before. From that day on, whenever she heard other cats whispering strange fears about boxes, she just laughed — because she knew better.

Quick Bridge

And hypnosis is the same. It’s not mind control, it’s not sleep, you’re never stuck, and you never lose your dignity. It’s simply a safe space to use your imagination — and once you experience it, or you remember seeing someone else experience it, those old fears just fade away.

Keep it conversational. Pick any 2–3 items, observe, utilize, then move smoothly toward your induction.
